  • Journal Papers
    1. Develop a non-reference visual quality assessment algorithm for wireless videos that suffer from data packet losses.
    2. Using this algorithm, the smart phones can automatically predict the perceptual quality of the received/corrupted video without referring to the original/uncorrupted video at the video server. The smart phone thus can request necessary but not excessive transmission resources (e.g. power or bandwidth) from the service provider to achieve great perceptual video quality for users.
    3. Implement an algorithm which measures the blockiness of encoded video frames without referring to the original video content.

  • Conference Papers
    1. Work on the project of "Perceptual Optimization of Large Scale Wireless Video Networks (VAWN)", Co-funded by Intel and Cisco.
    2. Conduct subjective study and investigate how to provide great quality of experience (QoE) to users of video streaming services.
    3. Propose a dynamic model which can accurately track users' QoE while a rate-adaptive video is streamed to the users.
    4. Propose a video rate-adaptation algorithm and a user admission control algorithm that incorporate wireless video users' quality of experience. The proposed scheme can reduce network resource consumption by 40% over conventional rate-adaptation algorithms.
